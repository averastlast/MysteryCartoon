Show Info
Dipper Pines
"Grunkle Stan, whenever I'm in those woods I feel like I'm being watched ... Something weird is going on in this town."

Dipper Pines is a smart and curious 10-year old spending the summer at his great uncle (grunkle) Stans's tourist trap, the Mystery Shack, with his twin sister Mabel. In comparison to Mabel, Dipper is the less outgoing of the pair and has a nerdy side. When he finds a mysterious and anonymously written journal in the woods that documents the odd and paranormal of Gravity Falls, Dipper's summer goal is to find The Author. His longing for adventure sometimes backfires though, as things in Gravity Falls sometimes are not as they seem, and leads to certain... situations.

Mabel Pines
"Check it out Dipper, I successfully bedazzled my face! Blink! Ow..."

Always ready to take the world by storm Mabel Pines is a force of nature with a bold, free-spirited personality and a heart of gold. She's excited to spend the summer at Mystery Shack with her brother Dipper. Mabel loves to wear many different styled sweaters that she makes herself, made fast friends with Gravity Falls locals Candy Chu and Grenda Grendinator, and has a pet pig named Waddles! While Mable doesn't always see eye to eye with Dipper, she truly cares about her brother and has fun hanging out with him. As long as there isn't a cute boy distracting her.

Grunkle Stan
"For tonight's final illusion we have the Incredible Sack of Mystery. When you put your money in it, it mysteriously disappears."

Great Uncle Stan is the owner and main tour guide of Gravity Falls' one and only Mystery Shack. One part home and one part weird specimens and oddities museum, the Shack is how Stan, the ultimate conman, makes his income. Stan often abuses the fact that he's an old man to get out of unsavory situations that he himself has gotten into. His love of money and questionable business practices rivals that of Mr.Krabs. Loves his nephew and niece Dipper and Mabel, but is by no means a role model for them, having a very loose sense of morality.
